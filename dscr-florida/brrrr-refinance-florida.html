BRRRR Refinance in Florida: Hard Money → 30-Year DSCR

The DSCR refinance is the "R" that makes BRRRR work — pulling your capital back out and locking in long-term financing on the rent.

BRRRR — Buy, Rehab, Rent, Refinance, Repeat — lives or dies on the refinance. You bought with hard money or cash, renovated, and got it rented. Now a 30-year DSCR loan pays off the short-term debt, returns your capital, and qualifies on the new, stabilized rent — not your tax returns.

How the DSCR refinance step works

  1. Stabilize: finish the rehab and sign a lease (or document market rent).
  2. Appraise: the new value reflects the renovated, rented property.
  3. Refi: a cash-out DSCR loan pays off hard money and returns equity.
  4. Repeat: redeploy the capital into the next deal.

The numbers that matter

  • Seasoning: often ~3–6 months before cash-out on the new value (varies by lender; delayed financing can be sooner).
  • Cash-out LTV: commonly ~70–75% of the new appraised value.
  • DSCR: the new rent must cover the new 30-year payment (PITIA).
  • Credit: typically 620–680+.
Run the renovated rent and your expected loan amount through the DSCR calculator to confirm the refi will pencil before you start the rehab — that's how you avoid a trapped deal.

Documents for the refi

  • Lease (or market-rent appraisal / Form 1007)
  • Payoff statement from the hard-money lender
  • Rehab receipts / scope (some lenders)
  • Bank statements for reserves
  • LLC docs (if vested in an entity)

FAQ

How soon can I refinance?

Often ~3–6 months seasoning for cash-out on the new value; some programs allow delayed financing sooner.

How much can I pull out?

Commonly up to ~70–75% of the new appraised value, as long as the rent supports the DSCR.
