DSCR Loans for Airbnb & Short-Term Rentals in Orlando

Orlando and Kissimmee are two of the hottest vacation-rental markets in the U.S. Here's how to finance a short-term rental on the property's income — not your tax returns.

Theme-park tourism makes the Orlando–Kissimmee corridor one of the strongest short-term rental (STR) markets in the country. But conventional financing often chokes on STR income and self-employed write-offs. A DSCR loan solves that: it qualifies the property on its rental income, so your Airbnb's cash flow does the work.

Why DSCR fits short-term rentals

  • Qualify on the rental's income — no tax returns or DTI.
  • Close in an LLC to keep the asset separate.
  • Works for purchases, refinances, cash-out, and BRRRR.
  • Scales — finance multiple STRs without conventional caps.

What you'll typically need

  • 20–25% down (up to ~75–80% LTV)
  • 620–680+ credit score
  • 3–6 months of reserves
  • A market-rent analysis (and sometimes an STR income schedule / AirDNA-style data)
STR programs and ratios vary by lender and by city/county short-term-rental rules. The fastest way to see where your deal lands is to run the numbers — try the calculator.

Orlando & Kissimmee STR tips

  • Check zoning/HOA rules. Some Central FL communities (and certain Osceola County "short-term rental" zones near Davenport/Kissimmee) are STR-friendly; others restrict it. Confirm before you buy.
  • Use realistic occupancy. Lenders look at sustainable market rent — not peak-season headline numbers.
  • Factor full PITIA including taxes, insurance (often higher in FL), and HOA when estimating DSCR.

FAQ

Can I use Airbnb income to qualify?

Yes — a DSCR loan can qualify an STR on its market/short-term rental income instead of your tax returns.

Is the down payment higher for an Airbnb?

Usually 20–25% down; STRs can carry slightly different terms than long-term rentals. It varies by program.
