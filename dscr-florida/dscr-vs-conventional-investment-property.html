DSCR vs Conventional for Investment Property

Two ways to finance a rental — one qualifies on your income, the other on the property's. Here's how to choose.

Both can fund an investment property, but they qualify you in completely different ways. Conventional looks at your income (tax returns, DTI). DSCR looks at the property's income (does the rent cover the payment?). That single difference drives everything below.

Side-by-side

  • Qualifies on: DSCR → rental income · Conventional → your personal income/DTI
  • Income docs: DSCR → none · Conventional → 2 yrs tax returns, W-2s, pay stubs
  • Property limit: DSCR → effectively none · Conventional → ~10 financed properties
  • Vesting: DSCR → LLC OK · Conventional → personal name
  • Down payment: DSCR → ~20–25% · Conventional → ~15–25% (investment)
  • Rate: DSCR → typically a bit higher · Conventional → typically lower
  • Speed: DSCR → fast (no income underwriting) · Conventional → slower

Choose DSCR when…

  • You're self-employed or write off most of your income.
  • You already have several financed properties.
  • You want to close in an LLC.
  • You need to move fast on a deal.
  • It's a short-term/Airbnb rental conventional won't count.

Choose conventional when…

  • You have clean W-2 income and a strong DTI.
  • You're under the property-count cap and want the lowest rate.
Many investors start conventional and switch to DSCR once they hit the property cap or go full-time investing. There's no wrong door — the right one depends on your file. Run your numbers and I'll tell you which fits.

FAQ

Is DSCR better than conventional?

Better for self-employed, multi-property, LLC, and speed. Conventional can win on rate with clean W-2 income.

Is the DSCR rate higher?

Usually somewhat higher — the trade for no income docs, no DTI, no property cap, and LLC vesting.
