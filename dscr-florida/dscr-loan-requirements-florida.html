DSCR Loan Requirements in Florida (2026 Checklist)

Everything you need to qualify for a DSCR investor loan in Florida — the ratios, credit, down payment, reserves, and the exact documents lenders ask for.

A DSCR loan (Debt Service Coverage Ratio loan) lets real estate investors qualify on a property's rental income instead of personal tax returns or debt-to-income. If the rent covers the payment, you can qualify — even if you're self-employed or write off most of your income. Here's exactly what's required in Florida in 2026.

The core requirements at a glance

  • DSCR ratio: typically 1.0+ (rent covers the payment). Low-ratio programs go to about 0.75 with pricing adjustments.
  • Credit score: usually 620–680 minimum; higher scores unlock better rates and LTV.
  • Down payment / LTV: generally 20–25% down (up to ~75–80% LTV) on a purchase.
  • Cash reserves: commonly 3–6 months of payments (PITIA) in the bank.
  • Property type: single-family, condo, 2–4 units, and many short-term/Airbnb rentals.
  • Loan amount: programs from about $75K into the millions.
  • Vesting: you can usually close in an LLC — great for investors.
  • No income docs: no tax returns, W-2s, or DTI calculation.
Exact numbers vary by lender, program, property, and the strength of the file. The figures above are typical ranges — the fastest way to know where your deal lands is to run it.

How the DSCR ratio is calculated

DSCR = monthly rent ÷ monthly debt service (PITIA), where PITIA is principal, interest, taxes, insurance, and any HOA/association dues.

  • Rent $2,400 ÷ PITIA $2,090 = DSCR 1.15 → qualifies (rent covers the payment with cushion).
  • Rent $1,900 ÷ PITIA $2,090 = DSCR 0.91 → a low-ratio program may still work.

Documents you'll need

  • Government ID
  • 2 months of bank statements (to show reserves / down payment)
  • The purchase contract (for a purchase) or current mortgage statement (refi)
  • A lease or market-rent appraisal (Form 1007 rent schedule)
  • LLC operating agreement / EIN (if closing in an entity)
  • Homeowner's insurance quote

Notably missing from that list: tax returns, pay stubs, and W-2s. That's the whole point of a DSCR loan.

Who DSCR loans are best for

  • Self-employed investors who write off income on taxes.
  • Portfolio builders hitting conventional property-count limits.
  • BRRRR investors refinancing out of hard money into a 30-year loan.
  • Short-term rental owners in Orlando, Kissimmee, and across Florida.

Frequently asked questions

What credit score do you need for a DSCR loan in Florida?

Most programs start around 620–680. A higher score improves your rate and max LTV.

What DSCR ratio do I need?

1.0 means rent exactly covers the payment. Many lenders want 1.0–1.25; low-ratio programs go to ~0.75 with adjustments.

How much down payment?

Typically 20–25% down (up to ~75–80% LTV) on a purchase; cash-out refis are usually a bit lower.

Do I need tax returns?

No — DSCR loans qualify on the rental's income, not your personal income docs.
